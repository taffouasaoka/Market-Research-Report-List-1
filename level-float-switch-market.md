It operates by using a buoyant float, which rises and falls with the liquid level, triggering on/off electrical contacts to control pumps or alarms. <p><p>The level float switch market features two primary types: top-mounted and side-mounted switches. Top-mounted level float switches are installed on the tank's upper section, allowing for easy access and maintenance. They are ideal for smaller tanks where space is limited. In contrast, side-mounted level float switches are affixed to the tank's side, making them suitable for larger tanks where vertical space is constrained. Both types effectively monitor liquid levels, offering reliable operation across diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
