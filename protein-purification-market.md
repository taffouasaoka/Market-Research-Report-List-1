<p><p>The protein purification market comprises various techniques. Ultrafiltration utilizes membranes to separate proteins based on size. Precipitation involves adding reagents to cause proteins to form a solid phase. Chromatography separates proteins through interaction with a stationary phase, offering high specificity. Electrophoresis separates proteins based on their charge and size in an electric field. Western blotting detects specific proteins using antibodies after electrophoresis. Other methods include affinity purification and ion-exchange chromatography, broadening the market's scope for diverse applications.</p></p>
