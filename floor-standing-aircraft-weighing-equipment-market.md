<p><p>Floor-standing aircraft weighing equipment comes in two primary types: digital and analog. Digital weight systems use advanced electronic sensors to provide precise weight measurements, often featuring user-friendly displays and data logging capabilities. They offer accuracy and ease of use, making them popular in modern aviation. Analog weighing systems rely on mechanical components like springs and levers to determine weight, providing a more traditional approach. While generally less precise than digital systems, they are often more durable and require minimal maintenance.</p></p>
