<p><p>The Plastic Optical Fiber (POF) market consists of two primary types: PMMA and Perfluorinated Polymers. PMMA, made from polymethylmethacrylate, is widely used due to its excellent optical quality, flexibility, and low cost, making it suitable for consumer electronics and lighting applications. Perfluorinated Polymers, on the other hand, offer superior performance in terms of lower attenuation and higher thermal stability, making them ideal for demanding environments such as telecommunications and data transmission networks. Both types serve distinct market needs.</p></p>
